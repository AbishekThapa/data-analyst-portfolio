Queensland Crash Analysis Findings

Comprehensive findings from the analysis of Queensland road crash data (2001-2024) revealing key insights and patterns.

Key Findings Summary

  • Peak Crash Times: Friday afternoons (3-6 PM) show the highest crash frequency
  • Geographic Hotspots: Major roads and intersections in urban areas are crash-prone
  • Fatal Factors: High-speed limits and poor lighting conditions significantly increase fatality rates
  • Weather Impact: Most crashes occur in clear weather due to higher traffic volume
  • Seasonal Patterns: Specific months show higher crash frequencies

1. Temporal Analysis Findings

Key Discovery: Crash frequency peaks significantly on Friday afternoons, with the highest concentration between 3-6 PM. This pattern suggests a strong correlation with the typical work week and rush hour traffic.

2. Geographic Distribution

Key Discovery: Crashes are heavily clustered along major roads and intersections, particularly in urban areas. The analysis identified specific suburbs with the highest crash counts, likely major commercial or transport hubs.

Critical Finding: The average number of fatalities per crash increases dramatically with speed limits. Additionally, "Darkness - Not Lighted" conditions are by far the most dangerous for fatalities.

8. Data Quality and Limitations

  • Reporting Bias: The data only includes crashes that were reported. Minor incidents may be underrepresented.
  • Data Accuracy: There may be errors in the recorded data. Missing values in key columns limit the scope of certain analyses.
  • Correlation vs. Causation: This analysis identifies correlations but does not prove causation.
  • Sample Size: The analysis covers a comprehensive dataset but may not capture all crash types.

9. Recommendations for Future Analysis

Based on these findings, future analysis could explore:

  • Crash Rate Analysis: Normalize crash counts by traffic volume or road length to find truly dangerous areas.
  • Severity Modeling: Build a predictive model to identify the factors most indicative of a severe or fatal crash.
  • Holiday Impact: Analyze the specific impact of public holidays on crash frequency and location.
  • Time Series Forecasting: Use historical data to forecast future crash counts.
  • Driver Behavior Analysis: Investigate the relationship between driver demographics and crash patterns.

10. Business Impact and Applications

Practical Applications: These findings can be applied to:

  • Traffic Management: Optimize traffic light timing and road maintenance schedules
  • Emergency Response: Position emergency services based on crash hotspots
  • Insurance Risk Assessment: Develop more accurate risk models for different areas and times
  • Public Safety Campaigns: Target awareness campaigns during peak crash times
  • Infrastructure Planning: Prioritize road improvements in high-risk areas
